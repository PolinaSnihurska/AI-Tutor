## Getting Started

### Creating a Parent Account

1. Visit the platform homepage and click **Sign Up**
2. Choose **Parent** as your account type
3. Enter your email, create a password, and fill in your information
4. Verify your email address
5. Complete the parent onboarding process

### Linking Your Child's Account

There are two ways to link your child:

#### Option 1: Child Has Existing Account
1. Go to **Settings** â†’ **Children**
2. Click **Link Existing Child**
3. Enter your child's email or username
4. Your child will receive a notification to approve the link
5. Once approved, you'll have access to their cabinet

#### Option 2: Create Account for Child
1. Go to **Settings** â†’ **Children**
2. Click **Add New Child**
3. Fill in your child's information
4. Create their account credentials
5. The account is automatically linked to your parent cabinet

## Understanding Analytics

### Performance Metrics Explained

#### Overall Performance Score (0-100)
Calculated from:
- Test scores (weighted by difficulty)
- Consistency of study habits
- Learning plan completion rate
- Improvement over time

**What it means:**
- 90-100: Excellent, on track for top performance
- 75-89: Good, solid understanding
- 60-74: Fair, needs more practice
- Below 60: Needs significant support

#### Improvement Rate
Shows how quickly your child is progressing:
- Positive %: Getting better
- 0%: Maintaining current level
- Negative %: May need intervention

#### Consistency Score
Measures regular study habits:
- High: Studies regularly, good routine
- Medium: Somewhat irregular
- Low: Sporadic studying, needs structure

### Heatmap Interpretation

The heatmap shows where your child struggles:

- **Red Topics**: High error rate (60%+ mistakes)
  - Action: Needs focused practice and possibly tutoring
  
- **Yellow Topics**: Moderate errors (30-60% mistakes)
  - Action: Needs additional practice
  
- **Green Topics**: Low errors (<30% mistakes)
  - Action: Mastered, maintain with periodic review

### Success Predictions

The AI predicts your child's likely exam performance based on:
- Current performance trends
- Study consistency
- Time remaining until exam
- Historical data from similar students

**Confidence Level:**
- High (85%+): Prediction is very reliable
- Medium (70-84%): Reasonably reliable
- Low (<70%): Too early or insufficient data

**Using Predictions:**
- Identify if current trajectory meets goals
- Understand what factors most impact success
- Get recommendations to improve predicted outcome
- Track how predictions change over time

## Supporting Your Child

### Recommendations Engine

The platform provides personalized recommendations:

#### For Weak Topics
- "Focus on [topic] - 3 practice sessions recommended"
- Suggested resources and lessons
- Recommended study time allocation

#### For Study Habits
- "Increase study consistency - aim for daily sessions"
- "Take more breaks - sessions are too long"
- "Balance subjects - spending too much time on [subject]"

#### For Motivation
- "Celebrate achievement: [milestone reached]"
- "Encourage practice in [subject] - showing improvement"
- "Exam in 2 weeks - increase study time"

### How to Help

#### When Performance is Strong
- Celebrate achievements
- Encourage continued consistency
- Consider advancing to harder material
- Maintain motivation with goals

#### When Performance is Declining
- Review the heatmap for specific weak areas
- Check if study time has decreased
- Look for patterns (time of day, specific subjects)
- Consider adjusting the learning plan
- Reach out to teachers if needed

#### When Child is Struggling
- Focus on one subject at a time
- Break study sessions into smaller chunks
- Use AI explanations for difficult topics
- Take easier tests to build confidence
- Consider professional tutoring for persistent issues

### Communication Tips

#### Discussing Progress
- Focus on improvement, not just scores
- Celebrate effort and consistency
- Use specific data: "Your math score improved 15%!"
- Avoid comparisons to siblings or peers

#### Setting Goals Together
- Review predictions and set realistic targets
- Break big goals into smaller milestones
- Track progress toward goals weekly
- Adjust goals as needed

#### Encouraging Independence
- Let your child own their learning plan
- Gradually reduce monitoring as they mature
- Praise self-directed study
- Use controls as safety net, not micromanagement

## Tips for Success

### Best Practices

1. **Check In Regularly**: Review dashboard 2-3 times per week
2. **Focus on Trends**: Don't overreact to single test scores
3. **Communicate Openly**: Discuss progress with your child
4. **Adjust Controls**: Loosen or tighten based on responsibility
5. **Celebrate Wins**: Acknowledge improvements and achievements

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Micromanaging**: Too much monitoring can reduce motivation
2. **Comparing Children**: Each child learns differently
3. **Ignoring Weak Signals**: Small declines can become big problems
4. **Over-Relying on Predictions**: They're guides, not guarantees
5. **Neglecting Communication**: Data is useful, but talk to your child

### Age-Appropriate Monitoring

#### Elementary (Ages 6-11)
- High monitoring and controls
- Focus on building habits
- Celebrate all progress
- Keep sessions short

#### Middle School (Ages 12-14)
- Moderate monitoring
- Encourage independence
- Focus on consistency
- Discuss goals together

#### High School (Ages 15-18)
- Light monitoring
- Respect privacy
- Focus on exam preparation
- Support, don't control
